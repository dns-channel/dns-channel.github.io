New root hints

Use dig, drill or kdig to fetch them from any root server into whichever file is used to prime your server, e.g., dig @198.41.0.4 > named.cache

Hints, full zone and trust anchor (KSK) files are available from or via IANA using FTP or HTTPS.

The "B" root server will change its IPv4 address from 192.228.79.201 to 199.9.14.201 on 24-October-2017. The old address will continue to respond to queries for at least 6 months. Most people do not need to do anything as a result. Resolver operators may wish to ensure that new root hints are obtained sometime after the change.

New root DNSSEC KSK

There are plans to replace (roll) the root's trust anchor (KSK, the key used to sign the root zone) in October 2017 -- it has been postponed (see their announcement). For most this will be a non-event, with the details taken care of by the entity providing them with DNS resolution. ICANN has information for anyone interested, especially those running validating resolvers.

Recursor services

Note that most ISPs and some datacenters provide it for free to their customers - we don't attempt to enumerate them here. Beware: some ISPs log and sell their resolver data, and some replace NXDOMAIN with their own server's address(es) to provide their form of safety and/or marketing, some do both.

Also, paid filtering services usually provide customization, so domains or classes of domains can be added or removed from the filters.
